The Controversial Demo Fee Policy!

Here’s an explanation of our somewhat controversial Demo fees. We charge you for a 20 minute demo of any of our audio products. The fee is kept as a store credit for you if you choose to buy the product, or any high end audio product, from us, ever. We’ve decided this is the only way we can resell high end audio; our business keeps us very busy, and we just don’t have the time to offer demos to anyone who walks in the door. We just aren’t that kind of shop. After 7 years of retailing these products, we found that our demo to resale ratio was crazy, it was unsustainable. Probably because everyone wants to hear Maggies and Ottawa hasn’t had a dealer in many many years. The question was, discontinue offering these products, or find something workable for us. We think this is workable, and will also allow us to demo more gear as well. We do realize this will alienate some customers, but the policy is in place so that we can continue offering these products. Better to serve customers who understand why this is necessary than not serve any customers at all and lose what’s been a decent product line for us. Here’s the fee structure:

20 Minute Demo, Under $1000-----2%

20 Minute Demo, Over $1000-----1%

We’re obviously not going to retire on these fees. But they should do what we put them in place to do. And if you’re a tried and true customer, don’t worry,we won’t be charging you any fees.

Trade In/Sell Your Mac

We love trade-ins, used Apple products hold up well and offer good value in the resale market. They also get you closer to your new Mac than you might think. Because our demand for used Macs is strong, we’re able to offer much better pricing than the 50% of retail value (as determined by Ebay’s Terapeak) that is the typical trade in offer. We are able to pay 70 to 85% of retail, because we do a strong volume in used Macs. Which is to say, we make it up with volume.

If you aren’t quite ready to get a new Mac, or you’re (shudder) considering going over to another platform, we do buy Apple products for cash money. Let’s face it, not everyone wants to wade through the low ball offers or crazy people emails Kijiji ads can generate. And you don’t have to. Our offers are very close if not better than what you may get selling privately, the process is fast and simple, and wham, you have a cheque in hand. Again, we use mactosell.net to establish retail value, and pay between 70 to 85% of that value for working condition and in good shape products. If a product is older than five years or so, or it’s a low demand product, or, for whatever reason, we feel it will take longer for us to resell it, we may offer less.
